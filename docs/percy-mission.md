# Percy Mission

Percy exists to turn business presentations and visual reporting from static files into living, programmable systems.

PowerPoint, PDF decks, Tableau workbooks, screenshots, charts, tables, and recurring executive reports are where organizations communicate decisions, strategy, performance, and narrative. But those artifacts are usually disconnected from the data, code, templates, brand rules, and institutional memory that created them. Teams repeatedly copy, paste, resize, recolor, and rebuild the same materials by hand, even when the underlying information already lives in data warehouses, spreadsheets, dashboards, APIs, and Python workflows.

Percy is building the missing operating layer between data, AI, and business storytelling.

The core idea is a Bridge model: a structured, inspectable, reproducible representation of business visuals. Existing decks, PDFs, Tableau workbooks, and templates can be onboarded into Bridge elements. Those elements can then be refreshed from Python, data pipelines, reusable templates, and AI agents, rendered back into familiar outputs, and visually compared against expected results.

The goal is not merely to automate PowerPoint. The goal is to make the organization's visual communication system computational.

In a mature Percy workspace, a company can:

- onboard its existing decks, dashboards, templates, and reporting artifacts;
- recover reusable layouts, charts, tables, styles, and brand components;
- bind visual elements to Python, SQL, APIs, spreadsheets, and warehouse data;
- schedule refresh jobs that update recurring reports automatically;
- preserve visual quality through rendering, diffs, diagnostics, and review workflows;
- let AI agents safely operate on structured business visuals instead of brittle screenshots or opaque Office XML;
- maintain team memory about what each slide, metric, component, and report is for;
- export polished outputs to PowerPoint, PDF, web, BI tools, and future channels.

PowerPoint remains an important output surface because organizations already rely on it. But Percy makes the deck an output, not the source of truth.

Python is the right foundation because it is where modern data work, automation, analytics, and AI already happen. By rebuilding the presentation layer in Python, Percy can connect business artifacts to the systems that produce truth, while remaining accessible to developers, analysts, data teams, and AI agents.

Percy's long-term mission is to become the enterprise platform for living business documents: reusable, data-connected, AI-operable, versioned, testable, refreshable, and ready for human review.
